üìú Game Rules üìú
  1. Team Composition: The game will consist of three to four teams. Each team must have exactly three members, ideally representing a cross-section of generations: one Baby Boomer, one Millennial, and one Gen Z. To encourage family bonding, a team must not consist solely of a single nuclear family.
  2. The "Buzzer" System: To answer a question, a team must loudly shout their Team Name. The first team heard by the host will be given the floor.
  3. Answering and Clues: If no teams can provide the correct answer, the host may provide a clue. After the clue is given, all teams will be granted a second chance to buzz in and answer.
  4. Scoring & Victory: The team with the highest total points at the end of the final round wins the Grand Prize.
  5. Runner-up: The team with the second-highest score will receive a Consolation Prize.
  6. Fair Play Policy: To keep the competition fair, any coaching or shouting of answers from the audience (non-players) is strictly prohibited. Furthermore, the use of smartphones or any internet-connected devices to search for answers is not allowed. Any team caught receiving outside help will have the points for that round forfeited.
  7. The "Huddle" Rule: Teams have a maximum of 10 seconds to "huddle" and finalize their answer before speaking. This ensures the Gen Z and Boomer members actually talk to each other!
  8. The "Respect the Host" Rule: The Host's decision is final. Any "pagrereklamo" (complaining) results in a 50-point deduction!
